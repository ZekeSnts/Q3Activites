About Fire Prevention Month

Fire Prevention Month is observed annually in March. It aims to raise awareness and educate people on the importance of fire safety to prevent fire accidents. According to the National Fire Protection Association (NFPA), fire departments in the United States responded to an estimated 1.3 million fires in 2020, which resulted in 3,655 civilian fire deaths, 16,850 civilian fire injuries, and $14.8 billion in property damage.

It is essential to take preventive measures to reduce the risk of fires and protect lives and property. Fire prevention starts with education, and this is the main goal of Fire Prevention Month. By educating people on fire safety, we can prevent fire accidents and save lives.


Causes and Effects of Fire

From Fireline UK, it was reported that fire-related fatalities have increased. The number of deaths has increased within a year. There were 261 deaths in 2016/17 while there were 334 deaths in 2017/18.

Smokers' materials have the highest fatality rate with 20%. However lighters and cigarettes are not the most common source of ignition of fires which is 7%.

Why you should be cautious

You never know when a fire may occur, it catches everyone off-guard. To ensure that you are prepared, always follow the said tips from below and practice fire drills at home, school or your workplace.

Fire is not only caused naturally but it can also be committed by a person. Arson is the act of setting fire to buildings on purpose. It is also one of the most common causes of fires.

Fire Prevention Tips

Tip Description
Install Smoke Alarms Install smoke alarms on every level of your home. Test them during fire drills and replace batteries if needed.
Cooking Safety Stay in the kitchen when you are frying or grilling food. Turn off the stove if you will not use it anymore.
Electrical Safety Don't overload electrical outlets or extension cords.
Fire Escape Plan Have a fire escape plan and practice it with your family. Know at least two ways out of every room.
